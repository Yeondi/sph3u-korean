Notebook problems, Learning Activity 2.8

Suggested answers

  1. A child is pushing a wooden block with a mass of 0.250 kg to the left, across a wooden floor. The child is applying a force of 4.0 N. The coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.20.
    1. Create a free body diagram (FBD) for the block. You should have four forces acting on it.
    1. Calculate the net force on the wooden block.

      Given:

      m = 0.250   kg

      μ k = 0.20

      F A = 4.0   N

      Unknown:

      F n e t =   ?

      Equation:

      F f = μ k F N

      Solve:

      Gravity and the normal force are equal in magnitude, so they cancel out in the vertical direction. Let [left] be the positive horizontal direction.

      F n e t = F A + F f

      F N = m g = ( 0.250   kg ) ( 9.8   N / kg )

      F N = 2.45   N

      F n e t = 4.0   N - ( 0.20 ) ( 2.45   N ) (since friction is always in the opposite direction of movement)

      F n e t = 3.51   N

      Statement:

      The net force on the block is 3.5 N [left].

    1. Find the acceleration of the wooden block.

      Given:

      F n e t → = 3.51   N [left]

      m = 0.250   kg

      Unknown:

      a → = ?

      Equation:

      Newton’s second law, which is F n e t → = m a →

      Solve:

      a → = F n e t → m

      a → = 3.51   N   [ left ] 0.250   kg

      a → = 14.04   m / s 2   [ left ]

      Statement:

      The block will accelerate at 14 m/s² [left]

  1. A rubber puck with a mass of 152 g is sliding south across wet asphalt. It is being slowed down by a frictional force of 1.4 N. What is the coefficient of kinetic friction?

    • Given:

      m = 0.152   kg (convert to kilograms)

      F f = 1.4   N

      Unknown:

      μ k = ?

      Equation:

      F f = μ k F N

      F N = F g = m g

      Solve:

      F f = μ k m g

      μ k = F f m g

      μ k = 1.4   N ( 0.152   kg ) ( 9.8   N / kg )

      μ k = 0.9398 (The coefficient of friction does not have units)

      Statement:

      The coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.94.
